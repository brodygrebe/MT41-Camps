Why “41”?

Montana is the 41st state, and that number represents a mindset that’s built into the people who live here. MT41 is about embracing that Montana Tough mentality — doing the little things right, pushing through challenges, and showing up for your team when they need you. It’s about building athletes who are confident, resilient, and prepared to lead.

  • Mental & physical development
  • Scheme‑free fundamentals every coach approves
  • Competitions spread throughout to build poise under pressure

What Athletes Work On

  • Footwork, leverage, safe tackling form
  • Offense focus: QB/WR/RB/OL mechanics
  • Defense focus: DB/LB/DL reaction & pursuit
  • Mental lessons: goals → accountability → leadership

Meet Coach Brody Grebe

Born and raised in Class C Montana, Brody Grebe understands what it means to represent a small community with big expectations. He grew up playing 6-man football in junior high before moving into 11-man football in high school, proving that talent, toughness, and work ethic can come from anywhere. That Montana-built mindset took him to the collegiate level, where he became a 2-time team captain, recognized as a leader by both teammates and coaches. Brody was named the Big Sky Defensive Player of the Year, is a 3-time All-American, 3-time First-Team All-Conference selection, 3-time Academic All-American, and 3-time Academic All-Conference honoree — all while earning a 3.96 GPA in Mechanical Engineering and being recognized as the Montana Association of Engineers Engineer of the Year. Dedicated to building leaders on and off the field, Brody is committed to giving Montana athletes the tools, mindset, and work ethic they need to succeed at the next level.

Sample 3‑Hour Camp Flow

Warm‑Up

Dynamic mobility, activation, speed prep.

Fundamentals

Agility, ball security, blocking fits, non‑contact tackling form.

Competitions

It's a secret, be ready for anything.

Offense Focus

QB drops & release, WR releases & breaks, RB vision & security, OL footwork & hands.

Defense Focus

DB backpedal & break, LB shuffle & angles, DL get‑off & hand usage.

Competitive Situations

1v1 & 2v1 non‑contact tackling fits; 1v1 OL vs DL pass rush.

FAQ

Who can attend?

Athletes entering grades 7–12.

Is there contact?

No live contact. Tackling instruction is non‑contact form and bag work only.

What should athletes bring?

Cleats, water bottle, athletic gear, sunscreen (outdoor), and a great attitude.
